There is genuinely no native collection export

This isn't a hidden setting or a permissions issue. Shopify's admin lets you export products, orders, and customers to CSV, but it offers no export for collections — smart or manual. The list of collections you see in the admin is the only view; there's no "Export" action behind it. If your premise when you landed here was "surely there's a native way," the honest answer is no, and everything below is about the ways around it.

The "Collection" column is a one-way street

The products CSV does have a Collection column, and it's easy to assume it round-trips. It doesn't. That column is import-only: on the way in it assigns products to a manual collection, but it is dropped from the products export — you'll never see it in a file Shopify gives you. And it only ever referred to manual collections: smart collections, which are defined by rules rather than a fixed product list, were never expressible in that column at all.

Manual and smart collections both need to come out

A complete export has to handle two different shapes. Manual collections are a curated list — title, handle, description, image, and their hand-picked membership. Smart collections carry no fixed list; they're a set of conditions (tag equals, price greater than, vendor is), and those rules are the thing you actually need exported — a membership snapshot alone loses how the collection is defined. An export that only dumps titles isn't an export you can rebuild from.
