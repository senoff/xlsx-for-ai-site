How to clean the file before you upload

Upload your file here, pick which columns hold private information — emails, phone numbers, names, Social Security Numbers (SSNs), addresses, bank account numbers, whatever you want gone — and we blank out those cell values. Everything else stays intact so the file is still useful for your analysis question. Then download the scrubbed version and upload that to ChatGPT (or whichever AI tool you're using). The AI sees the structure, the numbers, the dates, and the patterns — but it never sees the real people those rows represent. Ask your question, get your answer.

Common scenarios where this matters: sharing a customer list with a freelancer, preparing a file to post as an example on a forum, sending payroll data to an accountant who only needs the totals, or using AI to analyze a file your company policy says you can't share externally.

The problem nobody warns you about

When you upload a file to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any other AI tool, every value in every cell goes to that company's servers. That includes the things buried in columns you might not think about: a customer name column three sheets over, the email address column your export always includes, the Social Security Numbers in an HR report you're trying to analyze for headcount trends. The AI doesn't need any of that to answer your question about totals or patterns — but it gets sent anyway.

What this tool actually does (vs. what Microsoft's tool does)

If you searched "remove personal information from Excel" and found Microsoft's Document Inspector — that removes hidden file properties like who authored the document. It does not touch the data in your cells. The names and numbers in your spreadsheet are completely untouched by that tool. This tool removes the actual cell values.

What gets removed — your call entirely

We don't auto-detect what counts as "personal." You know your file; you tell us which columns to clear. We blank them, not delete them — the column headers stay in place and the rows stay the same count, so the structure the AI will analyze is faithful to the real file. Nothing is changed silently; you see the before and after before you download.
